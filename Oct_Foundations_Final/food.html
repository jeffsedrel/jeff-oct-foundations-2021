Bolognese

Here I've included a recipe for Pork Bolognese, one of my favorite sauces to make. It pairs well with just about any kind of Italian noodle, though I prefer rigatoni.
If you don't like pork this recipe substitutes well with just about any meat (I often will do a pork/lamb mix), and even meat alternatives.

Ingredients

1/4 lb Pancetta, small cubed
1 small yellow onion, finely diced
2 medium carrots, finely diced
1 stalk celery, finely diced
2 tablespoon green bell pepper, finely diced (optional)
2 pounds ground pork
1/4 cup tomato paste
1 tablespoon brown sugar
1 cup dry white wine (I use chardonnay)
3/4 cup milk
1/2 cup heavy cream
1 28oz can tomato sauce
2 cups chicken broth
1 bay leaf
7 sprigs thyme
pinch crushed red pepper
pinch dried oregano
pinch cinnamon
3 teaspoons salt
1/2 teaspoon pepper
grated parmesean
italian parsley

Directions


Step One

In a large stock pot cook pancetta over medium/medium-high heat until fat is released, about 5-7 minutes. Add in onion, carrots, celery, and bell pepper. Cook until soft. Raise heat to medium-high/high; add pork and cook until browned, breaking up as it cooks. Add tomato paste and coat the pork, then add brown sugar and coat as well, cook about 5 minutes.

Step Two

Add white wine, cook until reduced by about 3/4. Add milk and reduce by about 1/2. Add tomato sauce, broth and seasonings, and reduce to simmer. Stir in heavy cream and let simmer 1-2 hours. Serve with noodle of choice, grated parmesean, and parsley.
